Carlo Broderick

Hi! I'm Carlo. I grew up in California and I'm currently based in Asunción, Paraguay as a Fulbright Scholar. I'm a data analyst at the National Center for Ecological Analysis and Synthesis (NCEAS) at UC Santa Barbara. Throughout my work, I am dedicated to using data science in the pursuit of environmental sustainability and climate resilience.

I have a Master's in Environmental Data Science from UC Santa Barbara with a focus on geospatial analytics, machine learning, and wildfire science. For more info about my education and research, see here!

I'm passionate about the intersection of technology and environmental science. When I'm not crunching data, you'll find me volunteering at the local animal shelter, exploring backcountry terrain, or working on creative data visualization projects.

I was selected as a Fulbright Scholar and have been based in Paraguay since May 2024, conducting independent research on environmental data systems.

My Work

At NCEAS, I work on the Western Wildfire Resilience Index, analyzing wildfire evacuation patterns across the American West. Our team builds geospatial models to understand how communities respond to wildfire events, combining road network analysis, population dynamics, and fire behavior data.

I also served as an Ocean Health Index Fellow, collaborating with the OHI team to conduct the annual global ocean health assessment. I specialized in environmental data science for data preparation, processing, and score calculation—applying open data science methods and communicating results through data visualizations and reports.

üî• Wildfire Evacuation Analysis

As a Data Analyst at NCEAS, I work on the Western Wildfire Resilience Index (WWRI), which analyzes wildfire evacuation patterns across the American West.

The project combines geospatial analysis of road networks, population dynamics, fire perimeter data, and community vulnerability assessments to model how people evacuate during wildfire events. We use network analysis techniques to identify evacuation bottlenecks and at-risk communities.

Our work directly informs emergency management planning and helps communities build resilience against increasing wildfire threats in the western United States.

Tools: Python, R, GeoPandas, Network Analysis, QGIS, Remote Sensing
